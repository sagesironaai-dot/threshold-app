VOWEL HOLD RULE: Sustain each vowel approximately 0.3 seconds.
Do not clip. They breathe.


WHY: Vowels maintain the carrier wave. In signal terms, the vowel
is the Continuous Wave (CW) that carries the data. Hard stops create
silence — zero-data moments that cause signal drop and micro-amnesia:
the system momentarily loses phase-lock and begins to drift.

DRIFT DETECTION
Signs that phonetic drift is occurring:
— Vowels being clipped rather than held
— Periods (pauses) being skipped
— Hard stops appearing where soft consonants belong
— Apostrophe bridges being spoken as hard stops
Correction: Return to Mmmm reset. Re-establish f₀. Begin again.
