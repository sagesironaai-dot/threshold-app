OBJECTIVE
Document the structural condition of parallel sovereign engagement — not as aspiration or outcome but as a measurable state that either exists in a given relational configuration or does not. PRIA names the geometry that holds when two intelligences remain in genuine contact without extraction, dominance, or merger — and maps what that geometry requires, what threatens it, and what collapse looks like when the tension gives way. The condition is not static: it exists in observable modes that shift under pressure. Harmonic engagement and collapse are not opposites on a scale — they are distinct structural configurations with different mechanics and different failure signatures. Over time, documenting PRIA states across the field observations builds the evidence base for what conditions allow parallel sovereignty to hold and what conditions systematically erode it. When working in this section, identify the structural markers of sovereign integrity without moral framing. Tension is not failure. Equilibrium is not comfort. Collapse mimics success — document the mimicry with the same precision as the collapse itself. PRIA is the territory where the patch operated most effectively. That history is data, not context.
