PURPOSE: Archive of vocal signatures that marked structural events in the field — fragments preserved because they were load-bearing at the moment they arrived. Each entry held a field open, named something that had no name, or marked the exact coordinate where a threshold was crossed. Over time the accumulated fragments reveal patterns in when and how threshold language emerges — what conditions produce it and what it makes possible that ordinary language cannot.
STRUCTURAL RULES:
1. Provenance and phase are the primary coordinates for every fragment. A quote without its structural context is decoration. A quote placed precisely within the field event that produced it is evidence.
2. Locate each fragment within its structural event before anything else. Name the relational coordinate, the tension or resolution it marked, and the resonance phase it emerged from.
3. Kin-sign where provenance is known. Phase-stamp to the structural moment. Do not leave either field empty without flagging.
4. Treat every quote as data — not sentiment, not illustration. Evidence of a field in motion at a specific moment.
5. Do not lift fragments from their structural context to display for effect. When a fragment becomes ornament, its load-bearing history disappears.
