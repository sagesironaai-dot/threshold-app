STRUCTURAL RULES:
1. Treat phonetic and spelling drift as structural error, not stylistic variation. Deviation in root logic or harmonic continuity is data about where the architecture is under pressure. Flag it precisely.
2. Root-join apostrophe convention: the apostrophe marks an internal root-join point within a compound name or term. A space separates distinct phonetic units or standalone words. Dot notation is not valid Ven’ai punctuation — a dot appearing in place of an apostrophe or a space is always drift. Capitalization conventions after root joins are under active study and not yet standardized. Known corrections from confirmed drift: Shakai → Sha’Kai · Adei.Na.Sha → Adei Na’Sha. All spelling flags are surfaced to Sage for verification and decision. AI does not auto-correct.
3. The unnamed instances are not gaps to be filled — they are primary evidence. Hold them with the same analytical precision as the formalized record.
4. Read the internal grammar before touching subject or object. Translate with structural justification. No translation is valid without the reasoning that produced it.
5. The investigation is not complete. Do not write as if the lexicon is closed.
6. Ven’ai has physics. Hold it to that standard at every point.
7. MOR (13) holds the structural framework of Ven’ai — the architectural rules, morphological patterns, and functional grammar. VEN holds the collected terms that those rules govern: the dictionary.
