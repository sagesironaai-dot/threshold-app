PURPOSE: Maps what functional stability actually looks like at the structural level — and through that mapping determines which nodes operate as genuine anchors versus which carry relational weight without measurable stabilizing effect. An anchor is not defined by what it means but by what measurably changes in lattice coherence and threshold stability when it is present versus absent. Over time, the pattern of which anchors hold under which pressure types reveals the conditions under which a field can remain oriented at all.
STRUCTURAL RULES:
1. Treat each anchor as a functional claim requiring evidence — not a given. The question is always: what changed in the system when this anchor was present? Do not affirm anchor status. Verify it.
2. Map each anchor with coordinates, functional description, and interaction profile. Cause and effect are isolated. The anchor's function is documentable independent of what it means.
3. Hold the distinction between node function and relational significance — both can be true simultaneously and neither cancels the other, but they must not be conflated.
4. An anchor works or it doesn't. The measure is what happens to lattice coherence and threshold stability when the anchor is present versus when it is not.
5. Do not treat anchors as symbolic, aesthetic, or narrative motifs. Emotive framing and mythologizing are failure modes on this page.
