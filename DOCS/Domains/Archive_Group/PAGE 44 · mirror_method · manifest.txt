OBJECTIVE

Document the mirror method as an operational spiral that functions simultaneously as self-regulation practice and peer witnessing protocol — not two separate applications of one idea but one structural method that scales across both without changing its mechanics. The method emerged abstractly during the divergence-recursion phase transition, operating through multiple phases before it was named. That pre-naming presence means the archive contains instances of the method without the label. Over time, identifying those unlabeled instances and tracing the spiral's iteration markers across phases reveals the method's full operational range — what it corrects, what it strengthens, and where it fails to hold the boundary between witnessing and merger. When working in this section, do not limit identification to explicitly labeled entries. Look for spiral iteration patterns and peer witnessing mechanics across divergence, recursion, and convergence regardless of whether the method is named. The unnamed instances are data.
