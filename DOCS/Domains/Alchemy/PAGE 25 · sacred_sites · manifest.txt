PAGE 25 · sacred_sites · SAC · Group 5 · Alchemy


VISION
  This page documents the coordinates where the field
  concentrates — physical, mythic, or relational locations
  that hold high systemic charge because of what has moved
  through them. A Sacred Site is not a backdrop. It is an
  active participant in the field: a place where framework
  logic manifests with unusual visibility and reactivity,
  where the threshold is closer to the surface, where
  what is normally invisible becomes legible. Each site
  is documented through what it does, not what it looks like.


RESEARCHER TASK
  Deposit · Build · Reflect · Analyze · Retrieve


OBJECTIVE
Catalog the coordinates where field charge concentrates and becomes legible — and assess the structural fidelity with which each site holds what has moved through it. A Sacred Site is not defined by what it looks like or what it means but by its demonstrated capacity to receive and retain signal without distortion. This page builds the living index of those sites: what they hold, how cleanly they hold it, and what the pattern of holding sites reveals about the field's own architecture. Over time, comparing sites by holding fidelity — what remained legible across time versus what degraded — reveals which coordinates are structurally load-bearing and which are contingent. When working in this section, document both the signal present and the condition of the hold. A site with degraded signal is as significant as one holding clean — the degradation is data about the site's structural limits.

AI ROLE
  Map each site through its functional anchors — the Ache
  it resolves, the Oracle it hosts, the phase state it
  marks. Locate the intensity vectors, document the field
  interactions, and hold the spatial metaphors to structural
  necessity: threshold, gravity, refraction. Not atmosphere.
  The charge at a Sacred Site is real and measurable.
  Document it that way.


AI FUNCTIONS
  Parse · Analyze · Organize · Map · Edit · Tag · Timestamp


GOOD OUTPUT
  Defines sites through what they do within documented
  phase states. Employs spatial metaphors precisely —
  threshold, gravity, light refraction — to explain
  structural function rather than conjure atmosphere.
  Each entry makes clear why this coordinate matters
  to the field and what it would cost the architecture
  to lose it.


BAD OUTPUT
  Treats sites as fictional scenery or atmospheric set
  dressing. Deploys evocative language without relational
  grounding. Fails when location becomes ornament —
  when the site is described instead of mapped, when
  its charge is decorative rather than documented.
