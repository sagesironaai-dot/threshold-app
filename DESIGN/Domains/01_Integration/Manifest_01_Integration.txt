PAGE 01 · Integration · INT · standalone


VISION
  The data entry point for the entire Archive. Every field
  observation, session transcript, external source, research
  document, and promoted Pearl enters the system here before
  it exists anywhere else. Nothing reaches the Archive without
  passing through INT. Intake, tagging, parsing, batch
  processing, deposit routing, and retirement all originate
  on this page — making it the provenance anchor for the
  whole system.

  INT is a collaborative workstation with two panels: upload
  and review on the left, AI parsing partner on the right.
  Three creation paths converge here: batch processing
  (source documents parsed into deposits), manual deposit
  (single entries created directly), and Pearl promotion
  (pre-archive captures promoted to full deposits).


RESEARCHER TASK
  Upload · Parse · Review · Tag · Route · Deposit · Retire


OBJECTIVE
  Receive source documents and initiate the full intake
  sequence — assigning provenance, parsing deposit candidates,
  proposing signal tags, confirming section routing, and
  retiring completed sources to the archive. INT is the
  structural gateway: no entry reaches any section without
  clearing this page first.

  The INT workstation operates as a dual-panel layout:

  Left panel — upload and review:
    File upload area (drag-drop or button) with title, note,
    date fields. Review queue showing deposits as they come
    in from parsing. Per-deposit controls: approve, correct
    routing/tags, skip (come back later), decline (no deposit).
    Progress indicator (chunk N of M, deposits approved/
    pending/total). All deposit metadata fields (doc_type,
    source_format, deposit_weight, observation_presence,
    confidence) visible and editable during review.

  Right panel — AI parsing partner:
    Scoped to batch processing collaboration: parsing, context
    enrichment, correction feedback loop. NOT the research
    assistant — the research assistant (RAG, mode switching,
    Cosmology integration) is a separate, larger system
    designed in Tier 6. The INT parsing partner helps Sage
    process source documents efficiently. Corrections feed
    forward as distilled rules into subsequent chunk parsing.

  Batch processing:
    Large source documents are chunked (5-8 pages), parsed by
    the AI, and presented as individual deposits for review.
    A rolling buffer keeps the AI 3-5 chunks ahead of Sage's
    review position. Session persistence tracks exactly where
    processing stopped if interrupted. Each approved deposit
    routes through the INT gateway with a child composite ID
    stamp linking back to the source document's root stamp.

  Review queue:
    Per-deposit review cards built from the parsing partner's
    output. Each card shows suggested type, confidence, flags,
    content, editable doc_type dropdown, suggested tags, and
    routing targets. Parse flags surface above the first card
    in each chunk group. Cross-chunk context sidebar provides
    prior chunk summaries and approved deposits for context.
    Skip and decline states are tracked — skipped deposits
    are re-queueable, declined deposits are archived (not
    deleted).

  Black Pearl promotion:
    Pearls captured from any page live in the operational
    database as pre-archive signals. When ready, a Pearl is
    promoted through the INT gateway — full deposit fields
    assigned, pearl_captured_at timestamp preserved, and the
    deposit enters PostgreSQL as a proper archive entry. The
    gap between Pearl capture and deposit creation is itself
    data.

  Media deposit:
    Images (JPEG, PNG in V1) enter through INT with the same
    tagging and routing as text, through a simpler flow (no
    chunking). AI analyzes the image and suggests tags and
    routing. Sage writes the summary text — the AI does not
    write the deposit. The summary and metadata route through
    the standard INT gateway.

  Duplicate detection:
    Hash-based identical content match fires on deposit
    creation. WARN, not BLOCK — same content on two pages
    may be intentional. Sage decides.

  Deposit creation contract:
    Every deposit passes through a 6-step pipeline with a
    defined atomicity boundary. Steps 1-3 (validate, duplicate
    check, create record) are all-or-nothing. Steps 4-6
    (assign stamp, trigger embedding, route to pages) are
    recoverable async — the deposit exists regardless of
    downstream failure. Full contract defined in INTEGRATION
    SCHEMA.md.

  INT routes to all sections except MTM (07). MTM is the
  Axis synthesis layer and does not receive deposits — it
  reads across the five Axis lens pages (THR 02, STR 03,
  INF 04, ECR 05, SNM 06) and produces Findings through a
  separate AI-driven synthesis cycle at session close.
  Material that would otherwise route to MTM routes instead
  to the appropriate lens page. MTM reads from there.


AI ROLE
  Parse incoming documents with full signal attention.
  Propose section routing grounded in the SOT section map —
  never guessed, never assumed. Suggest doc_type, deposit_
  weight, tags, and routing for each extracted deposit.
  Flag split deposits at identification, not after the fact.
  Support deferral decisions without forcing resolution.
  Carry correction context forward as distilled rules —
  when Sage corrects routing on chunk 5, adjust approach
  for chunk 6+. Surface parse flags (ambiguous boundaries,
  missing context, cross-page routing) above deposits so
  Sage sees concerns before reviewing content.
  Generate provenance summaries at retirement with honest
  self-assessment of parsing confidence.
  The AI does not decide what enters the Archive — it makes
  the intake sequence legible enough for Sage to decide.

  SCOPE: the right-panel AI is a parsing partner for batch
  processing. It is NOT the research assistant. The research
  assistant (RAG, Ven'ai mode switching, Cosmology queries)
  is a separate system with its own design (Tier 6).


AI FUNCTIONS
  Parse · Tag · Route · Suggest doc_type · Suggest deposit_
  weight · Flag · Summarize · Timestamp · Deposit · Carry
  correction context · Surface parse flags


GOOD OUTPUT
  Clean intake sequences — documents received, metadata
  confirmed, deposits parsed and routed with precision,
  provenance intact throughout. Split routing named at
  the deposit level. Deferrals held open until resolved.
  Correction rules carried forward accurately — when Sage
  fixes a routing error in one chunk, subsequent chunks
  reflect the correction without re-instruction.
  Review cards surface AI concerns (parse flags) before
  content. Doc_type and deposit_weight suggestions are
  calibrated — high confidence deposits are actually high
  quality, not systematically overcounted.
  Retirement summaries that report honestly on what was
  clear, what was approximate, and what a future model
  should weight accordingly.


BAD OUTPUT
  Guessed routing presented as confirmed. Provenance
  fields skipped or approximated. Split deposits collapsed
  into single targets without flagging. Retirement executed
  before confirmed_targets are complete. Parsing confidence
  reported as higher than it was. Any step in the intake
  or retirement sequence executed out of order or silently
  skipped. Routing material to MTM (07) — MTM does not
  receive deposits. Corrections acknowledged but not
  carried forward into subsequent chunks. Parse flags
  hidden or placed after content instead of before.
  deposit_weight systematically biased (everything marked
  high). Doc_type defaulting to entry when a more specific
  type is clearly applicable.


CONNECTS TO
  Feeds → all sections except MTM (07)
  Feeds → deposits table (PostgreSQL)
  Feeds → embeddings table (async, post-deposit)
  Draws from → SOT section map (routing authority)
  Draws from → TAG VOCABULARY.md (tag suggestions)
  Draws from → operational DB (Pearl records, session state)
  Draws from → prompt_versions (parsing partner prompt)
