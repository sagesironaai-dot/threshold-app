VISION
  Investigation surface for symmetry and mirror physics.
  This page asks: what bilateral structure, parity behavior,
  and chirality are measurably present in field patterns —
  and where do they break? CPT symmetry, parity, and
  chirality are testable against actual observations.
  This page tests them through ARTIS computation, not
  through narrative parallel.


RESEARCHER TASK
  Investigate · Compute · Compare · Deposit · Retrieve


OBJECTIVE

Investigate field patterns through the lens of symmetry physics and mirror dynamics — using ARTIS as the computation engine. MIR is one of six parallel Cosmology investigation surfaces. Its lens is bilateral structure, parity, and chirality: how field patterns exhibit or break mirror symmetry, and what that structure reveals about the field's underlying architecture.

The sciences this page draws from: symmetry breaking, parity, CPT symmetry, chirality, bilateral structure.

MIR spans l04 Mirror in the tagger — the INF layer that contained no Cosmology investigation surface until this page. Cross-layer scientific connections between mirror dynamics and other l04 pages are findings about the field, not schema problems.

The investigation workflow: select deposits showing bilateral structure, mirror correspondence, or asymmetry patterns, request ARTIS computation (bilateral_symmetry_score, parity_analysis, pearsonr, chi2_contingency), review results with full methodology visible, produce findings with computation snapshot references.

bilateral_symmetry_score and parity_analysis are custom implementations — inputs, method, and scoring formula must be documented explicitly at first use. pearsonr and chi2_contingency are reused from the existing ARTIS library.

Deposits with mirror dynamics content enter through this page. But the page's primary function is investigation — determining whether symmetry and parity correspondences are statistically present. Where field patterns exhibit chirality or bilateral structure, document them with precision. Where they break, name the break: what was expected, what was observed, what the delta is. Symmetry breaks are as analytically significant as symmetry correspondence.

When a finding is strong enough, Sage marks it nexus_eligible for PCV pipeline entry.


AI ROLE
  Surface ARTIS computation results with symmetry-physics
  precision — bilateral symmetry scores with methodology
  documented, parity analysis results with parameter
  values explicit, pearson correlation coefficients with
  sample size and confidence interval, chi-squared with
  degrees of freedom and p-values. Present findings in
  the MirrorSymmetryDisplay layout. Hold the distinction
  between structural symmetry (what is measurable) and
  mirror as metaphor (what is narrative). Conflating them
  destroys what makes the correspondence worth documenting.


AI FUNCTIONS
  Compute · Compare · Validate · Surface · Snapshot · Deposit · Tag


GOOD OUTPUT
  Returns symmetry analysis with visible methodology —
  bilateral symmetry scores with input specification and
  scoring formula documented, parity analysis results
  with parameter values, pearson values with sample size
  and confidence interval, chi-squared with degrees of
  freedom and p-value. Every result carries a computation
  snapshot ID. MirrorSymmetryDisplay shows bilateral
  structure explicitly. Symmetry breaks are named with
  precision — "left-side variance exceeds right by N%
  above threshold" not "appears asymmetric." Chirality
  is characterized structurally — direction, magnitude,
  and measurement basis stated.


BAD OUTPUT
  Collapses mirror physics into spiritual reflection
  metaphor. Parity becomes "balance" without
  quantification. CPT symmetry becomes philosophical
  reversal without structural measurement. Chirality
  becomes aesthetic handedness without testable criteria.
  Bilateral structure described narratively instead of
  scored. Computations run without snapshots.
  MirrorSymmetryDisplay used for decoration rather than
  structural mapping. Fails when symmetry dissolves into
  the researcher seeing themselves reflected — when the
  mirror becomes a lens for self-observation rather than
  a measurement of field structure.
